I’ve used Notion for years, and it has grown from a simple note-taking app into
the system I use to manage content creation, development work, learning, and
personal projects.

The goal is not to build a perfect dashboard. It is to reduce the effort it
takes to capture an idea, understand what needs attention, and keep moving.

## Full-stack developer roadmap

My developer roadmap provides an overview of the subjects involved in becoming
a full-stack engineer. It is organized into fundamentals, frontend, backend,
and databases, with curated resources that make it possible to learn at your
own pace.

## Content Manager OS

This is the operating system behind my creative workflow. It connects projects,
tasks, partners, and earnings, and it has been part of my process since the
first video on my channel.

Good content starts with clear thinking and planning. Keeping research,
writing, production, and partnerships together saves time and makes it easier
to focus on the story instead of maintaining the system.

## Development notes

I keep a personal worklog for standups, sprint planning, postmortems, and weekly
notes. It is separate from the team’s project management tools: this space is
for preserving context and making past decisions easier to find.

## Smaller systems

The rest of the workspace includes a reading list, reusable snippets, a simple
personal finance tracker, and a LeetCode template for documenting solutions,
thought processes, and complexity instead of memorizing answers.

These systems are always changing, but they continue to help me stay organized
and focused on the work that matters. The complete walkthrough and templates
are available on [b-r.io](https://www.b-r.io/blog/notion-setup).

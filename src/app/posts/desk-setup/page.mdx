This is the companion guide to the video above, with every product link and photo in one place.

I'm a software engineer by day and a part-time creator in the evenings. I spend a lot of hours at this desk coding, editing, and learning, so I built a setup that stays minimal, functional, and productive for both.

## The Desk

The centerpiece is the [Ergonofis Sway Desk](https://shrsl.com/49346), which Ergonofis gifted me for this build. It's a game-changer: a slick control interface, an anti-collision system, and build quality that makes standing all day feel natural. They also sent their solid wood monitor riser / desk shelf, which keeps the display at eye level, improves posture, and adds structure to the surface.

- [Ergonofis Sway Desk](https://shrsl.com/49346): Versatile standing desk with intuitive preset heights
- [Ergonofis Desk Shelf](https://shrsl.com/49342): Solid wood shelf to elevate the display
- [Ergonofis Cable Management](https://shrsl.com/4933x): Keeps the underside tidy

## Monitors and Lighting

A high-quality monitor matters for my work. I chose the LG 34WN780 for its ergonomic stand and wide canvas, and added the BenQ ScreenBar Halo for consistent, glare-free light across the desk.

- [LG 34WN780 Monitor](https://amzn.to/3DJd86G): Strong monitor with ergonomic versatility
- [BenQ ScreenBar Halo](https://amzn.to/3fUAfCi): USB-powered lamp for consistent desk illumination

## Computer Setup

My primary machine is the 16-inch MacBook Pro. An Anker USB-C hub streamlines connections and cuts cable clutter, and a Nomad MagSafe dock keeps my phone out of the way.

- [16-inch MacBook Pro](https://amzn.to/41fkhEH): Reliable laptop for day-to-day work
- [Anker USB-C Hub](https://amzn.to/3Dk9vCV): Simplifies connections for a cleaner desk
- [Nomad MagSafe Dock](https://nomadgoods.com/products/stand-one-silver): Premium dock for MagSafe-compatible devices

## Audio

I prefer headphones over the monitor speakers. AirPods Max are my main pair for quality and focus, with AirPods Pro for lighter tasks and a Sonos Beam when I want the room to feel like a mini theater.

- [AirPods Max](https://amzn.to/3mie64b): Primary headphones for deep work
- [Apple AirPods Pro](https://amzn.to/3UmMQhq): Compact earbuds for lighter tasks
- [Sonos Beam Soundbar](https://amzn.to/46h5E6H): Wall-mounted soundbar for room-filling audio

## Peripherals

I'm a mechanical keyboard enthusiast. The Mode Envoy with Alexotos linear switches and a GMK Dracula keycap set is still my favorite daily typer. For pointing, I switch between a Logitech MX Master III and an Apple Magic Trackpad.

- [Orbitkey Desk Mat](https://amzn.to/3Dm37eu): Desk mat with built-in storage
- [Mode Envoy Keyboard](https://modedesigns.com/pages/envoy): Favorite keyboard of that year
- [Alexotos Linear Switches](https://modedesigns.com/products/alexotos-edition-switches): Smooth linear switches; I get a lot of compliments on the sound
- [GMK Dracula Keycap Set](https://geekhack.org/): Custom keycaps with a personal look
- [Logitech MX Master III](https://amzn.to/3U5syHG): Productive, ergonomic mouse
- [Magic Trackpad](https://amzn.to/46gxWyf): Gesture-friendly alternative to a traditional mouse

## Chair and Ergonomics

For long sessions, I chose the Herman Miller Aeron. Fully adjustable, with lumbar support that keeps posture comfortable through the day.

- [Herman Miller Aeron Chair](https://www.hermanmiller.com): Ergonomic chair built for long hours

That was the 2023 workspace. I've since moved and updated parts of the setup — see my [NYC desk tour](/posts/my-desk-setup-tour-in-nyc) for my next version, but this one still captures where I was that year: minimal, functional, and tuned for long coding and creating sessions.
